Why Golf?

Golf is unlike any other sport. You compete against the course, against yourself, and against every decision you've made from the first tee to the final green. It blends precision, patience, athleticism, and strategy into an experience that keeps players coming back for a lifetime.

The Four Majors

Every golfer dreams of winning a Major. These four tournaments represent the pinnacle of the sport and are played annually at some of the most storied venues in the world.

  1. The Masters — Augusta National, Georgia (April)
    • Only Major played at the same course every year
    • Winner receives the iconic green jacket
    • Par 72, 7,510 yards
  2. The U.S. Open — Rotates among elite U.S. courses (June)
    • Known for its brutal rough and narrow fairways
    • Tests precision above all else
    • Hosted at Pinehurst, Pebble Beach, Oakmont, and more
  3. The Open Championship — Links courses in the UK (July)
    • The oldest Major, first played in 1860
    • Links-style golf with wind as a major factor
    • St Andrews is the spiritual home
  4. The PGA Championship — Rotates among private U.S. clubs (May)
    • Often features the deepest field of any Major
    • Open to PGA professionals, not just tour members
    • Winner earns a lifetime PGA Tour exemption

Breaking Down the Swing

The golf swing is one of the most technically demanding motions in sport. It takes years to develop a repeatable, consistent swing — and even the pros are always tweaking theirs.

The Setup

Posture, grip, and alignment are the foundation. Poor setup leads to compensations throughout the swing.

The Backswing

Rotate the shoulders fully while keeping the lower body stable. The club should reach parallel at the top.

The Downswing

Initiate with the hips, not the arms. The sequence: hips → torso → arms → club. This generates power.

Impact & Follow-Through

Strike down on the ball with irons. Weight transfers fully to the lead foot through a balanced finish.
